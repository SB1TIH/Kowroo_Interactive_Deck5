01 / Blind Spot

The Corporate Blind Spot

Traditional Travel Risk Management systems are highly effective at monitoring operational threats, major incidents, and regional disruption. However, they often struggle to capture how risk is actually experienced by individuals on the ground.

The result is a critical visibility gap between what the corporate dashboard reports and what employees may genuinely feel, experience, or alter their behaviour around in real-world environments.

02 / Unseen Friction

The Price of Unseen Friction

One of the most overlooked costs in corporate travel is not the incident itself, but the subtle friction employees experience before, during, and after periods of heightened tension or uncertainty.

The illustrative chart below highlights a critical challenge: while operational assessments may stabilise or quickly return to "normal," employee confidence and behavioural recovery often follow a very different path. As individuals quietly adapt their behaviour, limit movement, avoid engagement, or manage perceived risks alone, organisations lose visibility into the realities of the employee experience on the ground.

Productivity

04 / In Action

Case Study: Dublin Civil Unrest (2023)

Following a stabbing incident outside a primary school in Dublin city centre on 23 November 2023, misinformation and anti-immigrant narratives rapidly spread online. Within hours, tensions escalated into significant public disorder involving arson, looting, attacks on public transport, and assaults on Gardaí.

Click through the timeline below to explore how traditional risk tools often miss critical windows of emerging and evolving risk, and how Kowroo captures the broader human and operational reality before, during, and after an incident.

1. The Build-Up - The Missed Early Signals

Following the stabbing, online hostility, misinformation, and anti-immigrant sentiment escalated rapidly across social platforms and messaging channels. While the physical unrest had not yet fully materialised, the surrounding risk environment was already changing.

Legacy systems largely waited for confirmed disruption or violence before triggering alerts. Kowroo’s contextual model instead identifies secondary indicators, rising community tension, targeted hostility, abnormal behavioural patterns, and rapidly evolving local sentiment, helping organisations surface emerging risks earlier.
